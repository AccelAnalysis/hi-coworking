# Hi Coworking Ops — Product Requirements Document (PRD)

**Version:** 1.0 (January 27, 2026)
**Product Owner:** Jonathan / Accel Analysis

## 1) Product description

HiSpaceOps is an operations and booking platform for a **micro, reconfigurable coworking space** that runs as a **six-seat shared coworking layout** during standard use and can be converted on demand into **podcasting, live talks, conference setup, or small meetings (10+ attendees)**. The platform is the **source of truth** for availability and prevents double-booking across mutually exclusive space configurations while enabling **member priority pricing and booking** alongside **public rentals at public rates** on a **first-come, first-serve basis** within configured public booking horizons.

---

## 2) Problem statement

Micro coworking spaces that host multiple use-cases are hard to operate with generic calendar tools. The core challenges are:

* Managing a **single physical space** with **multiple mutually exclusive configurations**
* Enforcing **member priority access** without creating manual scheduling conflicts
* Enabling **public rentals** while controlling risk (payments, waivers, deposits, COI)
* Coordinating ops needs (setup/teardown buffers, staff readiness, approvals)
* Ensuring reliable **calendar, scheduling, and user management**
* Supporting payments, reporting, integrations, and growth workflows (public → member)

---

## 3) Goals

1. Provide a single operational system that prevents **double-bookings** across all modes and resources, including setup/teardown buffers and holds.
2. Support clear, configurable business rules:

   * **Member pricing vs public pricing**
   * **Member priority booking** via advanced horizons and member-first waitlist notifications
   * **Public rentals** for any rentable mode at public rates within public booking horizons
3. Make booking and payment self-serve for both members and the public, while supporting approvals and compliance gating when required.
4. Equip staff/admin with real-time operational control: holds, approvals, day-of view, incidents, and automation.
5. Provide full reporting and integrations to support scale: calendar sync, access control, accounting, CRM, and marketplace syndication.

---

## 4) Success measures

* Zero double-booking incidents in production (including buffers and holds).
* 80%+ of bookings completed end-to-end without staff intervention.
* Accurate pricing applied to 99%+ of bookings (member vs public, modifiers, add-ons).
* Automated notification delivery success rate > 98% (email + SMS where opted-in).
* Utilization and revenue dashboards available for weekly/monthly reporting.
* Trackable conversion pipeline from public renters to members.

## 6) Assumptions and policy decisions

1. HiSpaceOps is the **source of truth** for availability.
2. **Member priority** is enforced via:

   * longer booking horizons for members
   * member-first waitlist notifications
3. Public rentals operate FCFS **within** a public booking horizon (public cannot block far-future member access).
4. The space can be in **one mode at a time**; mode bookings are mutually exclusive with other modes and seat bookings.
5. Certain booking types can require approvals, deposits, and documents based on configurable policies.

---

## 7) Personas

* **Member (Solo):** books coworking seats and occasional modes, expects member pricing and easy scheduling.
* **Member (Small Team):** books meeting/podcast blocks, may use company accounts, needs reliable billing.
* **Public Renter (Creator):** books podcasting with add-ons; wants fast booking and clear instructions.
* **Public Renter (Organizer):** books live talk/event; needs capacity management, deposit, and compliance flow.
* **Staff:** manages approvals, day-of execution, holds, incidents, and no-shows.
* **Admin/Owner:** configures pricing/rules/integrations and monitors business performance.

---

## 8) User journeys

### 8.1 Public booking flow

1. Discover resource/mode page (SEO)
2. View availability (within public horizon)
3. Provide required details (attendees, event type, add-ons)
4. Accept/sign required documents; upload COI if required
5. Pay (one-off; deposit if required)
6. Receive confirmation + calendar invite + reminders + access instructions (if enabled)

### 8.2 Member booking flow

1. Login
2. View priority availability (extended horizon)
3. Book seat or mode with member pricing
4. Receive confirmation + calendar invite + reminders

### 8.3 Staff ops flow

* Review approvals queue
* Use Today View for schedule + mode transitions
* Create holds/blocks
* Check-ins / no-shows
* Incident logging
* Trigger operational messages if needed

## 9) Functional requirements (FR) and acceptance criteria

### FR-1 Users, roles, identities

**Requirements**

* Support member and public accounts.
* Implement RBAC: Admin, Staff, Member, Public.
* Optional company accounts and team membership.
* Profile completeness rules per booking type.

**Acceptance criteria**

* A public user can create an account during checkout and manage bookings.
* Member pricing and priority availability are visible only to eligible members.
* Staff cannot modify pricing rules unless granted permission.

---

### FR-2 Membership plans, tiers, priority rules

**Requirements**

* Plans define booking horizons, eligible pricing, discounts, included credits (if used).
* Public and member horizons enforced server-side.
* Waitlist supports member-first notifications.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Public cannot book outside public horizon; members can book further out.
* A waitlist opening notifies members first for a configurable window.

---

### FR-3 Resource model for reconfigurable space

**Requirements**

* Resources include modes and seats; modes are mutually exclusive.
* Mode templates enforce capacity, buffers, required details, and required documents.
* Add-ons attach to applicable modes; equipment add-ons can be inventory-limited.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Booking Podcast Mode blocks Conference Mode and coworking seats in the same time window including buffers.
* Booking a seat blocks that seat and cannot overlap a mode booking.

---

### FR-4 Booking and scheduling engine

**Requirements**

* Booking types: hourly, half-day, full-day, recurring.
* Buffers applied automatically by resource.
* Policies: notice periods, duration limits, cancellations/no-show rules.
* Approvals: per resource, configurable.
* Conflict detection includes buffers and holds.

**Acceptance criteria**

* System prevents any overlap after buffers are applied.
* Cancellation inside policy window triggers configured fee and notifications.
* Approval-required bookings remain pending until staff/admin decision.

---

### FR-5 Pricing, billing, payments

**Requirements**

* Dual pricing (member/public) per resource.
* Advanced modifiers: peak/off-peak, weekend, demand-based adjustment.
* Stripe supports subscriptions, one-offs, deposits, refunds, credits.
* Coupons, gift cards, taxes, invoice templates.
* Dunning, grace, and auto-suspension on payment failure.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Total price at checkout matches the server-generated quote snapshot.
* Refunds and credits are reflected accurately in reports and audit logs.
* Payment failure triggers correct dunning sequence and entitlement changes.

---

### FR-6 Calendar and availability

**Requirements**

* Member portal shows extended availability; public shows limited availability.
* Google and Outlook sync supported for ops calendar and booking events.
* Holds/blocks override availability.
* Operating hours configurable by day.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Calendar events created/updated/deleted when bookings change.
* Availability reflects holds immediately.

---

### FR-7 Communications and automation

**Requirements**

* Email + SMS (opt-in) confirmations, reminders, receipts, doc reminders, and changes.
* Rules-based automations for booking types (podcast intake, event checklist).
* Broadcast announcements to members.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Confirmations and reminders fire reliably with correct booking context.
* Automation triggers are tied to booking type and executed consistently.

---

### FR-8 Contracts, waivers, and compliance

**Requirements**

* Document templates with auto-fill fields.
* Lightweight e-sign capture with audit metadata.
* COI upload requirement configurable by resource/booking type.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Bookings requiring documents cannot be confirmed until signed/uploaded (configurable).
* Signed documents are stored and retrievable by staff/admin.

---

### FR-9 Access, check-in, onsite operations

**Requirements**

* Self check-in via QR or portal.
* Staff Today View includes schedule, arrivals, and mode transitions.
* Incident logs linked to bookings and users.
* Access control integrations issue/revoke access in a time-bound manner.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Access is granted only for booking window (+ grace) and revoked on end/cancel.
* Staff can mark no-shows and create incidents with attachments.

---

### FR-10 Reporting and audit control

**Requirements**

* Utilization by mode and seats.
* Revenue split member/public, add-ons, deposits, refunds, fees.
* Conversion tracking public → member.
* Full audit trail for changes to bookings, policies, and financial actions.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Reports are exportable and match booking/payment records.
* Every admin/staff action that changes state is logged with actor and timestamp.

---

### FR-11 Marketplace features and demand capture

**Requirements**

* SEO-friendly resource/mode pages with real-time availability.
* Gift cards, review prompts post-visit.
* Marketplace syndication integrations.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Public pages are indexable and bookable.
* Syndication feeds/flows reflect availability accurately and safely.

---

### FR-12 Integrations and extensibility

**Requirements**

* Accounting sync (e.g., QuickBooks), CRM sync (e.g., HubSpot).
* Zapier/Make/webhooks and public API support.
* Integration health monitoring and retries.

**Acceptance criteria**

* Sync jobs are idempotent and retry-safe.
* Integration failures do not break core booking/payment workflows.

---

## 10) Non-functional requirements

* **Atomic booking creation** to prevent concurrency issues.
* **Idempotent webhook handling** (Stripe, calendar, partners).
* **Security:** server-side RBAC enforcement; least-privilege data access.
* **Performance:** availability queries fast (<500ms typical range).
* **Reliability:** scheduled jobs and retries for integrations.
* **Auditability:** immutable audit logs for key actions.

---

## 11) Platform screens (release set)

**Public:** resource pages, availability, checkout, booking management, documents, gift card purchase.
**Member:** dashboard, booking calendar, membership & billing, invoices/receipts, profile, waitlist management.
**Staff/Admin:** Today View, approvals queue, holds/blocks, resource/policy/pricing config, users, reports, incidents, audit logs, integration settings, marketplace settings.

---

## 12) Risks and mitigations

* **Concurrency double-booking:** enforce slot-locking transactions and server-only booking writes.
* **Calendar conflict drift:** nightly reconciliation + app as source of truth.
* **Public booking risk:** deposits + approvals + doc/COI gating; incident tracking.
* **Integration failures:** job queue with retries and idempotency keys.
